## Abilities

Abilities are assigned to caught Pokemon from `src/data/abilities.ts`. Main-game ability definitions are generated into `src/data/abilities/canonical-generated.ts` from the local PokeAPI CSV cache by `scripts/generate-canonical-abilities.mjs`; the active registry is currently canonical-only. The exported lookup map is derived from those generated canonical records. Runtime behavior resolves through normalized `AbilityEffect` data from `src/data/abilities/effects.ts`, with exact authored battle effects where supported and conservative best-effort battle approximations for every other canonical ability. Assignment comes from three pools:

- Global pool: currently empty.
- Type pool: currently empty.
- Form pool: `ABILITY_FORM_MAP` is built from ability metadata at load time. Canonical abilities use `naturalAssignments`, keyed by form ID, with normal slots resolving to a guaranteed base ability and hidden ability slots rolling first at 5%.

When a Pokemon evolves, it preserves the source Pokemon's natural ability slot on the evolved form. Matching target slots are used when available; otherwise, the evolved form uses the first target assignment with the same hidden/normal visibility before falling back to a natural target-form roll.

Canonical natural abilities now cover all generated Pokemon forms with source-data ability slots. Every canonical ability resolves to at least one battle effect; unsupported cartridge mechanics such as doubles redirection, true contact-only filtering, full cartridge grounded overrides from items/field effects, broad ability copying/masking, and move-flag-specific boosts are approximated with thematic effects supported by the current single-battle engine. `docs/game-data/ability-implementation-audit.md` tracks known bad approximations and remaining engine gaps. A curated set of canonical abilities also has conservative secondary Capture and Field Observation effects. These are additive to battle behavior and include small catch-rate/timer assists, success-only item or research XP rewards, Field Observation pool weighting, spawn visibility/count modifiers, global Pokemon event odds multipliers, extra collectibles, collectible duration/quantity boosts, reward protection, and small Field Observation research XP boosts. No current canonical secondary effect changes shiny odds, replaces encounters, changes encounter level, or fails encounters. Battle ability effects are active-Pokemon only: partner/companion abilities do not apply to a Pokemon that is not currently fighting.

AbilityDex registration is stored in `user-abilitydex-entries`. Wild catches register the rolled ability after the Pokemon is created. Pokemon granted through the reward system use explicit `pokemonData.ability` as an override, otherwise roll the rewarded form's canonical natural ability, and register the resolved ability so gifts and purchases unlock the same dex entry path. Registered AbilityDex entries keep the canonical ability description separate from game-specific Capture and Field Observation bonuses, which are shown as generated `Partner Effect` rows from the same runtime ability effects used by encounters.

Current ability-patch item status:

Ability patches are generated from every authored ability in `ABILITIES` as `{abilityId}-ability-patch` where `{abilityId}` is the ability slug with `_` converted to `-`. Abilities with `locked: true` mark special natural abilities that cannot be overwritten by another ability patch.

## Moves

Move data is custom and stance-based, not a full canonical Pokemon move list. `MoveConfig.damage` values are multipliers against the shared 50 BP battle baseline, so a 1x move resolves as 50 BP before stance, stats, type, weather, held-item, and other modifiers. `MoveConfig` supports stance, damage multiplier, defender-type damage overrides, weather-only damage multipliers, target, eligible Pokemon forms, forced type, optional type-effectiveness bypass, accuracy, always-hit accuracy bypass, buffs/debuffs, status effects, stance-disable effects, same-turn enemy AI move interruption, reusable attacker-vs-defender contests, move-level gates, secondary statuses, charged moves, recharge moves, continuous moves, delayed damage, and optional enemy AI use timing, terrain-setting effects, and explicit multi-hit metadata. Move configs also support reusable runtime effects for called moves, exact flat/current-HP/user-level/user-current-HP/last-damage-taken damage rules, dynamic typing such as held `{type}-plate` Judgment, held `{type}-memory` Multi-Attack, Drive-based Techno Blast, and weather-shaped Weather Ball, one-use next-damage and next-accuracy effects, conditional damage modifiers including random chance rolls, previous failed move, previous successful move, fainted party member count, user/target positive stat stages, user-no-held-item checks, target Pokemon type checks, user recently took damage, or target Dynamax checks, post-damage status cures, post-damage stat-stage effects such as target-KO boosts, weather-aware healing, stat-stage copy/swap/reset/inversion effects, type-filtered stat-stage boosts, status/party cures including target cures with optional user healing, main-status transfers, secondary-status cures, party revival, forced/pending switches, move locks, move-use depletion, battle conditions including held-item, target-held-item, consumed-held-item, user-status, target-status, first-active-turn, and gender requirements, random, first-known-move, target-primary, last-opponent-move-resistance, set/add/remove battle type changes, player-side battle rewards, held-item transfer/removal/theft/swapping/recycling/self-consumption/berry consumption, and per-side battle item-use availability changes. Move contests can compare level, HP, size, battle stats, effective battle stats, or friendship against the target or an authored number. Move visibility flags include `trainerOnly` for moves wild Pokemon cannot use, `aiOnly` for moves excluded from generated TMs/MoveDex/player assignment, and `manualOnly` for bespoke moves excluded from automatic enemy AI pools unless listed directly on a battle Pokemon; broad unauthored TM libraries now rely on missing/empty `formId` lists rather than `manualOnly`. Buff and debuff stages can include an optional `chance` (defaults to 100) and an optional `target` (defaults to `self` for buffs and `enemy` for debuffs). Player, PVP, and enemy AI move resolution all honor those buff/debuff targets. Main status effects can also include optional `chance` (defaults to 100), `target` (defaults to the move target), and `forceStatus` for moves like Rest that replace an existing main status. `statusTransfer` moves such as Psycho Shift copy the user's current main status to the target through normal status immunity checks, then clear the source status only after a successful transfer. `MoveConfig` also supports `damageRange`, `multiHit`, and `randomStatuses` for bespoke random effects and explicit multi-hit total damage ranges; immediate multi-hit damage resolves a hit count and applies damage in per-hit chunks so hit-sensitive shields, held blocks, Endure, and on-damaged hooks can trigger for each hit. Metronome-style called moves resolve into another authored move before execution, excluding themselves and moves that require follow-up locked turns. Hidden Power is runtime-special-cased instead of using canonical Pokemon formulas: IV parity resolves one of the non-Normal attack types, and average IVs scale its damage multiplier from 0.7x to 1.5x. Advanced and boss enemy AI can generate Hidden Power for any Pokemon form, while normal player assignment and lower enemy AI still respect Hidden Power's authored form list. Secondary statuses are runtime effects separate from normal status; they can target Pokemon, sides, both sides, or the field, trigger before moves, on turn end, or on switch-in, optionally delay before first effect, roll fixed or ranged durations, and apply damage/absorb with optional Pokemon-type filters, healing, regular status, status immunity, wake-status effects, stat stages with optional Pokemon-type filters, outgoing stance/type-specific damage modifiers, incoming stance/type-specific damage reduction or damage-taken modifiers, passive accuracy bypass, type-immunity bypass that makes matching 0x type effectiveness resolve as neutral 1x damage, infatuation, voluntary switch prevention, or move blocking by zero-damage moves, healing moves, repeated last-used moves, explicit move ids, or matching attack types with optional block damage. Reapplying the same secondary status ID to the same Pokemon, side, or field refreshes/replaces it instead of stacking duplicates. Pokemon-attached secondary statuses clear when that Pokemon switches out or when a move authors `secondaryStatusCure`; move-authored cures can target self, enemy, or both, choose Pokemon statuses, side statuses, or both, and optionally clear only named secondary-status IDs. Rapid Spin clears the user's Pokemon and side secondary statuses, while Tidy Up clears authored hazards plus Substitute/Shed Tail decoys from both active sides without removing unrelated effects. Foresight and Odor Sleuth apply an Identified secondary status that lets Normal and Fighting attacks hit Ghost targets neutrally; their canonical evasion reset is intentionally ignored because this game has no evasion stat. Miracle Eye applies the same immunity-bypass pattern for Psychic attacks into Dark targets. Attract applies gender-gated Infatuation to opposite non-genderless targets; infatuated Pokemon have a 50% before-move immobilization roll. Block, Mean Look, Spider Web, and trapping residual moves apply source-linked switch-prevention secondary statuses that block voluntary player, PVP, and trainer AI switching without blocking forced KO replacements or move-forced switches; those trapping effects end when the Pokemon that caused them leaves battle. Camouflage randomly changes the user's active battle type through `typeChangeEffect`; Conversion changes the user's active battle type to its first known battle move's type, Conversion 2 changes the user to a type that resists or is immune to the opponent's last successful authored move or basic stance attack type, and Reflect Type changes the user's active battle type to the target's primary type. Soak and Magic Powder set the target's battle type, Forest's Curse adds Grass, Trick-or-Treat adds Ghost, Smack Down/Thousand Arrows remove Flying, and Roost/Burn Up/Double Shock can temporarily or persistently remove a battle type until duration expiry or switch. Weather Ball is Normal/50 BP in clear weather, changes type from active battle weather, and doubles to 100 BP in non-clear weather. Magnitude uses random damage scaling from 0.2x to 3x. Rototiller creates a five-turn field secondary status that raises Attack and Special Attack when a Grass-type Pokemon switches in. Purify cures the target's main status, fails if no status was cured, and heals the user for half max HP only on a successful cure. Disable now authors `disableStance: { stance: 'random', turns: 2 }`, resolving to one of the target's base stances at use time. Pay Day adds a flat 30 Pokédollar player battle reward through `battleRewards`. Rage is approximated as weak Normal damage that doubles only after the user recently took damage, instead of granting an immediate Attack stage on use. Pledge moves and Fusion Flare/Fusion Bolt can scale from the user's previous successful authored move through a conditional damage modifier. Last Respects scales from fainted allies through `fainted-party-members`, keeping normal typed Ghost damage rather than fixed damage. Nightmare is sleep-gated and applies three turns of 25% max-HP end-turn damage. Curse uses a dedicated branch effect: Ghost users spend half max HP and curse the target for 25% max-HP end-turn damage, while non-Ghost users raise Attack/Defense and lower Speed. Charged moves use `charged` as the number of charge turns before release; charge logs stay generic and hide the move name/stance until release. Vanished charge moves such as Fly, Bounce, Sky Drop, and Phantom Force apply Vanished when charging starts and fail before spending the move if Vanished cannot be applied. Recharge moves use `recharge` as the number of turns after a successful release where the UI is locked to a Recharge action and the player skips attacking while the enemy acts. Continuous moves use `continuous: { min, max }`; after a successful first use, the battle locks the player into that move for the rolled remaining turns, repeating the same effect until the count ends, the move misses/fails, or it is interrupted. Continuous moves can also author `repeatDamage` for per-use damage growth, as Echoed Voice does with +100% per successful repeat up to five uses, and `continuousEnd.status` for effects that apply only when the continuous sequence naturally finishes, as Thrash, Outrage, Petal Dance, and Raging Fury do for self-confusion.
Move contests can compare effective battle stats, raw battle stats, level, current HP, max HP, height, weight, or size, then configure success/failure damage, battle result, failure behavior, and counterattack prevention. Moves can also author `preventCounterOnStanceWin` for stance-win-gated effects such as Sucker Punch without using a stat contest. Stance-fail rules can be authored with `failOnStance`; Protect, Detect, and Quick Guard are one-turn 100% damage-reduction secondary statuses that take hold without stance-loss gating. Specialty guard moves use one-turn 75% stance-specific damage reduction: Baneful Bunker and King's Shield protect against Power, Spiky Shield and Obstruct protect against Speed, and Wide Guard plus Burning Bulwark protect against Tech.

Owned Pokemon must have battle moves assigned from the Pokemon details menu before those moves appear in battle. Each Pokemon stores up to four selected moves in the `pokemon.assignedMoves` Payload field, but Researcher level controls how many slots are usable: 1/2/3/4 slots unlock at Researcher levels 10/20/45/60. Move IDs are authored as lowercase move-name slugs, and TM items are generated from those moves as `tm-{move-name}` with a standard description and forced-type sprite. The available assignment pool is built only from owned move-granting TMs/HMs, with move-level and form gates still enforced; authored TM/HM move-level gates are capped at level 50. Move configs no longer author Pokemon type eligibility; compatibility must be authored through explicit `formId` lists, and missing or empty lists mean no Pokemon can learn the move yet. In battle, the Power Up drawer and server execution first use assigned moves that are legal at the Pokemon's battle-capped level, then temporarily fill empty slots with AI-scored compatible moves from the player's owned TMs/HMs.

Owned Pokemon can hold one eligible item through `pokemon.heldItemId` once Trainer level 15 unlocks held items. Atk, Fling requires and consumes the user's held item, Foul Play uses the target's attack stat, Jaw Lock traps both active Pokemon, Lash Out doubles after same-turn stat drops, Memento and Parting Shot apply both listed stat drops, Obstruct adds the adapted defense drop, Payback/Power Trip/Punishment/Pursuit use conditional damage, Quash interrupts the target's same-turn action, Ruination halves current HP, Snatch steals supported beneficial self effects, Taunt blocks zero-damage moves, Throat Chop blocks authored sound move IDs, Torment blocks repeating the last successful move, and Wicked Torque uses a sleep chance. Remaining notes are intentional authoring comments for authored multi-hit damage ranges, battle-flow adaptations such as Pursuit using side-level switch intent, or flavor-only item text. Weather-setting entries are intentionally not authored because battle weather is controlled through the Weather Control power system. Electric Terrain, Grassy Terrain, Misty Terrain, and Psychic Terrain are authored as future TM moves with no learner or reward lists yet. Z-Move, Dynamax, and Gigantamax attacks from that source table are also intentionally not authored as TM/type moves because those mechanics are handled through Pokemon Powers.

Status effects used by moves and battle logic include paralysis, sleep, poison, bad poison, burn, freeze, frostbite, confusion, veil, regeneration, mystic veil, shield tiers, T-shield tiers, S-shield tiers, victory, and vanished. Stance-disable effects are stored separately from normal status so they can coexist with status conditions. Vanished now blocks incoming damage while active, then clears at the next selected attack and grants a 1.3x damage bonus on that attack. Regeneration heals 1/8 max HP at turn end. Bad poison ramps its end-turn damage, sleep blocks movement for a hidden 2-4 turn duration before waking, freeze blocks movement until a thaw check succeeds, confusion can make a Pokemon hurt itself before acting, and sunlight prevents new freeze applications.

## Battle Powers

Power data lives in `src/data/powers.ts`; item definitions live in key items and battle items. Power usage requires both the normal key item/item requirements and the relevant Trainer level. Power counters default to one use per battle, and battle configs can set explicit hard caps when content needs to lower that. The engine supports more than current progression exposes.

`vs-seeker` is rewarded by the one-time `pokemon-fan-club-expedition`. It is a reusable key item that appears at the top of Explore when ready and starts a dynamic 3v3 trainer battle every 20 minutes. It samples non-legendary, non-mythical enemy Pokemon from the player's seen Pokedex forms, gives each opposing Pokemon a 30% chance to hold an eligible berry, gives the trainer a 50% chance to bring one healing item, sets the player level cap to the generated trainer level, and uses standard trainer win rewards. Trainer healing item rolls start with Potion, add Super Potion after 4 Kanto badges, add Hyper Potion after 6, and add Full Restore after all 8.

Most current authored battles do not set battle-specific item, move, or power caps. `itemsPerBattle`, `movesPerBattle`, and the individual power counter fields remain available on battle config as hard caps against the normal Trainer-skill or one-use defaults. Player and PVE enemy move-use limits are applied per Pokemon at battle start. PVE enemy AI move uses default from the highest enemy Pokemon level using the same battle-move ladder as Trainer level; `enemyMovesPerBattle` remains available as an explicit per-enemy-Pokemon special-battle override.

PVE trainer battle configs can also author `trainerItems`. Each entry references an item and optional `quantity` defaulting to 1. Enemy AI scores configured trainer items from their battle effects, currently healing, status clearing, and battle-stat buffs; powers such as Tera and Z-Move remain player-only for enemy AI. Trainers can use at most one configured item per turn. Moves that change item-use availability interact with these assigned quantities for enemies and with current battle item availability for players, without changing the configured max item capacity. PVE battle enemies select up to four AI move IDs at battle start from compatible authored moves plus any explicit `enemyTeam[].aiMoves` manual exceptions. Per-turn decisions use one unified scorer across AI moves, items, and trainer switches, with move scoring based on damage, accuracy, type/weather effectiveness, healing, absorbs, buffs/debuffs, status effects, secondary statuses, self-damage, interrupt potential, and remaining move budget. Authored enemy moves roll their configured accuracy when executed in retaliation or enemy-only turns; misses skip damage, move effects, on-hit held effects, successful move history, and follow-up locks. Enemy-authored moves support battle conditions, effective-Speed contests, stance-disable effects, delayed damage queues, charged turns, recharge turns, continuous move locks, and self-pivot switches; `battleRewards` remain player-only. Normal basic stance attacks still roll from the original weighted stance percentages instead of countering the player's submitted stance, and move selection ignores that hidden current-turn stance before final stance multipliers resolve. Forced enemy replacements after a KO use the same matchup scorer without spending a turn, so trainers select the best available bench Pokemon by offense, incoming type risk, and HP instead of only checking broad type buckets. Damaging moves are hard-skipped when their resolved attack type would have no effect.

Dusk Mane and Dawn Wings Necrozma Ultra Burst through the Z-Move system: once a prepared Z-Move is actually used, the active fused Necrozma changes to Ultra Necrozma for the rest of the battle with Ultra Necrozma stats and typing. The stored owned Pokemon form is unchanged.

Player battle item use checks item `skillRequirements` before consumption. Trainer level controls item capacity per battle: 2 at level 1, 3 at 17, 4 at 38, and 5 at 52. Trainer level also controls trained move uses per Pokemon per battle: 2 at level 10, 3 at 21, 4 at 42, and 5 at 59. Current battle-use gates are Trainer level 1 for Battle Observer, Trainer level 3 for Potion, Trainer level 4 for Fresh Water, Trainer levels 5-9 for Antidote, Paralyze Heal, Awakening, Burn Heal, and Ice Heal, Trainer level 27 for Super Potion, Trainer level 29 for Soda Pop, Trainer level 34 for Lemonade, Trainer levels 31-35 for X items, Trainer level 36 for Dire Hit, Trainer level 37 for Full Heal, Trainer level 40 for Training Bands, Trainer level 41 for Sitrus Berry, Trainer level 51 for Hyper Potion, Trainer level 60 for Max Potion, and Trainer level 81 for Full Restore. Held berries also use Trainer item gates when assigned or used. Berries are not directly usable from the inventory or Pokemon item dialog; color/utility berries act as crafting materials, while eligible Oran/Sitrus/status berries act through battle item or held-item systems. Pokemon-targeted items can also author reusable form-change effects, currently used by Rusty Sword, Rusty Shield, Griseous Orb, Adamant Orb, Lustrous Orb, Rotom form manuals, Ogerpon masks, Reveal Glass, Gracidea, Prison Bottle, and Meteorite. Pokemon-targeted fusion effects are used by DNA Splicers, N-Solarizer, N-Lunarizer, and Reins of Unity; they fuse Kyurem/Necrozma/Calyrex with the selected owned partner, hide that partner from normal availability, and unfuse both records when the same item is used on the fused Pokemon. Held item assignment syncs Plate, Memory, and Drive form families for Arceus, Silvally, and Genesect. Assigned move changes also sync Keldeo between base and Resolute Form based on whether Sacred Sword is assigned, and Meloetta between base and Pirouette Form based on whether Relic Song is assigned. No berry grants direct friendship. Ball usage is not skill-gated; ball access is controlled by shops, drops, Artisan recipes, materials, and recipe/manual unlocks. Lures and repels are Explorer-gated outside battle, with Repel at 20, Super Repel at 40, and Max Repel at 75.

Owned Pokemon lose 1 friendship, clamped at 0, whenever they faint in PVE or PVP battles. NPC trainer and wild enemy Pokemon do not persist friendship changes.

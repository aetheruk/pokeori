# Battle System

Turn-based Pokemon battle system with advanced mechanics.

## Locations
- Routes: `/game/battles/*`
- Components: `src/components/game/battles/`
- Engine: `src/utilities/battle/engine/`
- Data: `src/data/battles/`, `src/data/powers.ts`

## Mechanics
### Battle Types
- **PvE**: Battle against gym leaders, wild Pokemon, NPCs
- **PvP**: Battle against other players (via `/game/battles/pvp/[id]`)
- Wild battle enemy entries can define `requirements`; the server filters locked enemies before rolling the wild opponent.

### Battle Powers
| Power | Requirement | Effect |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| Battle Shouts | Book of Shouts + Trainer 40 | Battle-configured shout effects |
| Weather Control | Weather Orb + Weather Core + Trainer 65 | Overwrite battle weather until another battle weather override |
| Terastallization | Tera Orb + stored Tera type + Trainer 70 | Change Pokemon type for 3 turns |
| Mega Evolution | Mega Stone + Trainer 75 | Transform for the rest of battle |
| Z-Moves | Z-Ring + Trainer 80 | Prepare a 5x normal stance attack for the next turn |

| Dimensional Shift | Orb key item + Trainer 95 | Time/space/chaos battle effects |

### Pokemon Setup
- Owned Pokemon use assigned battle moves rather than every available move. Researcher level unlocks 1, 2, 3, and 4 usable move slots at levels 10, 20, 45, and 60. The species also needs Pokemon Research level 1 to assign or use those moves. TM/HM moves are gated by ownership and authored `formId` lists, not Pokemon type arrays; missing or empty `formId` lists mean no Pokemon can learn that move until learners are authored.
- Owned Pokemon persist their own `battleKOs` counter. Battle resolution records KO credits when a Pokemon's recorded damage knocks out an opposing Pokemon, then writes those credits back to the Pokemon document when the battle ends or is surrendered.
- Regular PvE wild/trainer enemies roll missing IVs and EVs when battle starts. Wild Pokemon roll missing IVs once, use 0 EVs through level 40, and roll missing EVs from 1-100 above level 40. Trainer Pokemon roll missing IVs once below level 30, twice from level 30, and three times from level 50, keeping the best roll per IV; missing EVs roll from 1-70 below level 20, 1-150 from level 20, 50-252 from level 50, and 100-252 from level 70. Manual authored IV/EV values remain fixed.
- Assigned moves can author `charged` turn counts, `recharge` turn counts, or `continuous` min/max turn ranges. Charged moves spend generic charge turns before release without revealing the move name or stance. Recharge moves deal damage first, then lock the UI to a Recharge button for the authored number of turns; pressing it skips the player's attack while the enemy acts. Continuous moves lock the player into the same move button until the rolled duration ends or the move misses, fails, or is interrupted; while locked, normal stance attacks, items, switches, and other moves are blocked.
- Move configs can mark moves as `trainerOnly`, `aiOnly`, or `manualOnly` for bespoke cases. Broad TM libraries should remain normal move configs and use explicit `formId` authoring for learnability; missing or empty `formId` lists mean no Pokemon can learn that move, no generated TM is exposed, and no MoveDex assignment entry appears. When a battle Pokemon has `aiMoves`, that list is its complete special-move pool.
- Held item assignment unlocks at Trainer level 15 and Pokemon Research level 1 for that species. Type boost held items exist in three crafted tiers for all 18 Pokemon types: diminished versions give +10% matching-type damage with a 25% attack break chance, normal versions give +10% and never break, and Unstable versions give +15% with a 15% attack break chance; break chances only roll when the item actually boosts that attack's resolved type. Training Bands lower their matching battle stat by 15% while held, then silently grant 1 matching EV up to the 255 stat cap on battle wins with a 2% held-item consumption roll. Plate and Memory held items drive Multitype and RKS System active battle types, and battle-local item transfer, theft, swapping, removal, restoration, or consumption refreshes those types immediately. Eligible held items can trigger automatically in PvE and PvP. Consumed held items are unavailable for the rest of the active battle, then auto re-equip after battle only if another copy can be consumed from inventory. Move-caused held item transfer, theft, swapping, throwing, removal, and berry destruction are battle-only item state changes and do not consume inventory or permanently alter the Pokemon's assigned held item. Charge-based held items persist progress on `pokemon.itemCharge`; Inferior elemental stones gain 5% when hit by their matching attack type, reset when removed, and at 100% remove themselves to grant the matching evolution stone. Inferior Light/Dark Stones gain 2% from any damaging attack only during day/night windows; Light completes into Dawn Stone from 06:00-06:59 or Sun Stone otherwise, while Dark completes into Dusk Stone from 18:00-18:59 or Moon Stone otherwise.
- Pokemon Powers require the active Pokemon to have Pokemon Research level 3 for its species, in addition to the selected power and Trainer/key item requirements. Z-Moves use only the Z-Ring: preparing one spends the turn, the next normal stance attack shows `Z` instead of stat numbers and deals 5x damage, and the charge fades if another move or item is used first.
- Battles inherit the weather snapshot resolved when the encounter starts. Weather modifies damage in PvE, PvP, special moves, and battle powers: sun/rain adjust Fire and Water damage, extreme sun blocks Water, heavy rain blocks Fire, sandstorm boosts Rock Sp. Def and damages non-Rock/Ground/Steel active Pokemon, hail damages non-Ice active Pokemon, snow boosts Ice Defense, snowstorm combines snow's Ice Defense boost with hail-style chip damage, fog lowers non-perfect move accuracy, strong winds neutralize Flying weakness from Electric/Ice/Rock attacks, shadowy aura damages non-Shadow active Pokemon, and sunlight prevents freeze.
- Canonical main-game abilities are generated for all Pokemon forms. Battle runtime support covers active-Pokemon type immunities, type absorbs with healing/stat boosts, status immunities, Pokemon-type-gated status and stat-drop protections, mental effect protections, stat-stage protections/triggers/bypass, main-status reflection, main-status counter steps, priority-style move contests, terrain/weather setters and sub-rules, end-turn status cures, active battle type changes, held-item hooks, battle-win reward hooks, battle stat multipliers, final damage multipliers, ability type-effectiveness overrides, active ability copying, move and held-item scouting, field-scope move blocking, non-damaging status-move blocking/bounce logs, added-effect suppression/chance/damage modifiers on damaging moves, ability-granted same-turn interruption chance, post-damage attacker status riders, per-hit multi-hit damage application, extra-hit damage events, reactive chip and KO damage, full-HP survival, identity masking, and suppression, Wonder Guard, Disguise/Ice Face shields, and form-change abilities including entry and Tera-activation field hooks. Doubles redirection, true contact-only triggers, broad ability copying/masking, and move-flag-specific boosts remain unsupported or approximated.
- Moves can apply secondary statuses separate from the normal one-status slot. Secondary statuses can attach to a Pokemon, a side, both sides, or the whole field; they can trigger after normal end-turn damage/weather or when a Pokemon switches in. Reapplying the same secondary status ID to the same Pokemon, side, or field refreshes/replaces the existing instance instead of stacking duplicates. Pokemon-attached secondary statuses, such as Yawn-style delayed effects, are cleared whenever that Pokemon switches out; side and field statuses persist unless a move authors a side-scoped `secondaryStatusCure`. Supported effects include damage, absorb damage, healing, regular status application, status immunity, wake-status effects, stat stages with optional Pokemon-type filters, outgoing damage modifiers keyed by stance/type, damage reduction keyed by incoming stance/type, type-immunity bypass, infatuation, switch prevention, and move blocking. Foresight and Odor Sleuth use immunity bypass only; their canonical evasion-reset helper behavior is not separately modeled beyond standard accuracy/evasion stage logic. These effects log text only and do not add battle UI visuals.
- Moves can require stance wins with `failOnStance: 'tie'`; `preventCounterOnStanceWin` lets those stance-win moves also cancel the opponent's same-turn attack, as Sucker Punch does.
- Move configs also support reusable runtime effects for called moves, terrain setting, explicit multi-hit metadata, dynamic move typing, exact flat/current-HP damage rules, always-hit accuracy bypass for moves such as Kowtow Cleave, one-use next-damage and next-accuracy modifiers, conditional damage modifiers, post-damage status cures, post-damage stat-stage effects such as target-KO boosts, weather-aware healing, stat-stage copy/swap effects and type-filtered boosts, party/status cures, target status cures with optional user healing for moves such as Purify, secondary-status cures, party revival, forced or pending switches, move locks, target-status battle conditions, first-active-turn gates, held-item transfer/removal/theft/swapping/recycling/berry consumption, and battle item-use availability changes. Secondary statuses can also grant passive accuracy bypass for affected Pokemon while active. Secondary move-block statuses can block zero-damage moves, specific move IDs, repeated last-used moves, healing moves, or matching attack types with optional block damage. Stockpile-style item-use changes adjust current available battle uses only; they never create or consume inventory items or raise the battle's max item capacity.
- Player battle item use checks item skill requirements before consumption.
- Pokemon Research level 4 gives player-owned Pokemon a 2% chance to survive a KO blow at 1 HP if they are not already at 1 HP.

### Type Chart
Located in `src/utilities/battle/engine/type-chart.ts` - standard Pokemon type effectiveness.

Damage calculation uses a shared 50 BP baseline for normal stance attacks and 1x battle moves before stance, stats, type, weather, held-item, and other modifiers. It also includes reverse-STAB as a final multiplier: if the resolved attack type does not match any of the attacker’s effective battle types (including tera override), damage is reduced by 15%.

### Wild Battle Rewards
- Wild battles roll one base candy reward using the highest enemy level to choose the candy tier: XS/XS EX at 65%, S/S EX at 45%, M/M EX at 30%, L/L EX at 25%, and XL/XL EX at 15%.
- Badge support drops can add extra candy rolls. Owning the Cascade Badge adds a 20% S Candy roll when the highest wild enemy level is above 15.
- Owning the Rainbow Badge adds a 20% S Candy EX roll when the highest wild enemy level is above 25.
- Owning the Soul Badge adds a 20% M Candy roll when the highest wild enemy level is above 30.
- Owning the Earth Badge adds a 20% M Candy EX roll when the highest wild enemy level is above 40.
- Battle wins give each involved player Pokemon 1 Pokemon Research XP. Wild battle Pokemon gem rewards grant one guaranteed base-type gem per qualifying reward pass. Pokemon Research no longer adds extra candy rolls to wild battle wins.
- Owned Pokemon lose 1 friendship, clamped at 0, each time they faint in PVE or PVP battles. NPC trainer and wild enemy Pokemon do not persist friendship changes.
- Trainer battles guarantee 1 candy, using the highest level Pokemon on the opposing team to choose the candy tier.
- Authored early Kanto trainer battles use original Red/Blue-style prize money: trainer class base payout times the level of the trainer's last Pokemon.
- Losing or surrendering a normal trainer battle deducts up to half of that battle's configured PokeDollar win reward and shows the loss on the defeat result screen. Wild battles, PVP, chronicles, and reward-disabled battles do not apply this payout loss.
- VS Seeker battles appear at the top of Explore when the `vs-seeker` key item is ready after its 30-minute cooldown. The Explore modal uses the standard battle stat chips for generated enemy level and team size, and the standard reward carousel for the reward bundle. They use 3 non-legendary, non-mythical Pokemon sampled from the player's seen Pokedex forms, cap the player team at the generated trainer level, use the `advanced` enemy AI profile, and pay 1000 PokeDollars, 1 League Ticket, plus 3 of each level-up candy tier up to the generated trainer level.

## State Management
- `useBattleManager.ts`: Main battle state manager
- Event generator for battle log and ordered combat, KO, and switch/replacement animations.
- Successful damaging attacks include optional `playerAttackType` / `enemyAttackType` battle-log metadata. The visual queue uses that resolved type at impact time to show target-side type effects only for the side that wins the stance matchup, while keeping HP drops and damage splats synchronized with the existing combat sequence. The battle log keeps the existing server message contract but parses newline-separated messages and inline icon tokens into one-line attack rows with icon-only stance badges, type/effectiveness/damage details, separate effect rows, and flat turn sections on the log background. Battle controls use taller stance buttons, sprite-icon item/power triggers with inline item/move use counts, and an icon-only switch trigger. The player's Pokemon HUD sits at the bottom of the sprite stage.
- AI logic for PvE opponents. On voluntary player switches, PvE trainer decisions are based on the Pokemon that was active before the switch, then the selected attack resolves into the switched-in Pokemon.
- PVE enemy AI uses a unified action scorer for stance attacks, special moves, configured trainer items, trainer switching, and forced trainer replacements after a KO. At battle start each enemy selects up to four AI move IDs from compatible authored moves plus any explicit `enemyTeam[].aiMoves` manual exceptions, then spends that enemy Pokemon's move budget only when a selected move is actually used. Authored enemy moves roll their configured accuracy when executed; misses skip damage, move effects, on-hit held effects, successful move history, and follow-up locks. Enemy-authored moves support the same battle conditions, effective-Speed contests, stance-disable effects, delayed damage queues, charged turns, recharge turns, continuous move locks, and self-pivot switches as player-authored moves; player-side battle rewards remain player-only. Normal stance attacks roll through the original weighted stance formula instead of countering the player's submitted stance, and move selection no longer scores against that hidden current-turn stance; final stance multipliers still resolve after both sides have chosen actions. Basic player and enemy stance attacks are normalized as no-cost internal 50 BP battle actions, while authored moves carry a move id and consume the move budget. Move decisions score damage, accuracy, type/weather effectiveness, healing, absorbs, buffs/debuffs, status effects, secondary statuses, self-damage, interrupt potential, and move resource cost; move-level `aiUse` rules are no longer required. Enemy profiles add early/repeated move penalties and pace non-damaging effects against stance attacks, while damaging and valid called moves can still be selected when their own scoring and conditions justify them. Secondary statuses are not refreshed unless missing or expiring, Protect-style damage-reduction moves are held for meaningful incoming damage and cannot repeat consecutively, enemy-targeted sleep application is not intentionally selected while the target is already asleep, and Mimic/Copycat-style calls fail AI selection until there is a valid authored opponent move to copy. Trainer items are scored from their battle effects, powers remain player-only for enemy AI, and wild AI does not voluntarily switch. Basic stance attack data and stance multipliers remain unchanged.
- Mobile/PWA recovery: the client snaps to the latest confirmed battle state if visual animation timers stall, and re-fetches battle state if a server action stays in the resolving state too long.
